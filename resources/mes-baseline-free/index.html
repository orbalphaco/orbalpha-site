// MES BASELINE — FREE EDITION REPRODUCTION GUIDE

this guide walks you through reproducing the published baseline for ORB v2.2 Free on MES (Micro E-mini S&P 500). when you finish, your strategy tester output should match the published numbers exactly.

if it doesn't, the troubleshooting section at the bottom tells you how to find the difference.

// THE BASELINE

ORB v2.2 Free, MES1!, 5-minute chart, January 2019 – December 2025.

metricpublished baseline
net P/L+$12,360.50 (+24.72%)
max drawdown$5,447.75 (10.90%)
total trades1,629
win rate50.09% (816 / 1,629)
profit factor1.072

these numbers were generated by TradingView's strategy tester running the published Free indicator with the configuration described below. they are reproducible — the goal of this guide is to walk you through reproducing them yourself.

// WHAT YOU NEED
  • a TradingView account (free or paid)
  • the ORB v2.2 [ORB Alpha] - Free script added to your chart (publicly available on TradingView)
  • approximately 5 minutes of setup time
// STEP 1 — SET THE SYMBOL

at the top-left of your TradingView chart, change the symbol to:

MES1!

this is the continuous front-month contract for Micro E-mini S&P 500 futures. make sure you're on the futures symbol, not the index.

// STEP 2 — SET THE CHART TIMEFRAME

at the top of the chart, set the timeframe to:

5m

(5-minute bars). all baseline numbers are computed on this timeframe. running on a different timeframe will produce different output.

// STEP 3 — ADD THE INDICATOR

if you haven't already, click the Indicators button at the top of the chart, search for "ORB v2.2 [ORB Alpha] - Free" by ORBAlpha, and add it to your chart. you should see the indicator's stats panel appear on the right side of the chart.

// STEP 4 — OPEN INDICATOR SETTINGS

click the gear icon next to the indicator name in the chart legend (top-left of the chart) to open the settings dialog.

// STEP 5 — SET SESSION TIMES

in the Settings dialog, set the following:

  • session start: 09:00
  • session end: 10:00
  • ORB cutoff: 09:35
  • exit time: 16:00
  • timezone: America/New_York (NY session)

these are the indicator's default settings. if you haven't changed anything, they should already match.

// STEP 6 — SET RISK PARAMETERS

still in Settings:

  • target risk: $250
  • max risk per trade: $350
  • max contracts: 40

these are the default risk values for micro futures. they match the baseline exactly.

// STEP 7 — VERIFY ENTRY MODE

confirm the entry mode is set to:

ORB

(no Stretch — Stretch is a Pro-only feature and is not available in the Free edition. the Free indicator's only entry mode is ORB.)

// STEP 8 — SET THE DATE RANGE

at the top of your chart, click the calendar icon (or the date range selector) and set the range to:

January 1, 2019 – December 31, 2025

this is the full backtest window for the published baseline. the indicator will run on every bar in this range.

// STEP 9 — OPEN THE STRATEGY TESTER

at the bottom of the chart, click the Strategy Tester tab. you should see the strategy report for the ORB v2.2 Free script.

if you don't see anything, make sure:

  • the indicator is added to the chart
  • the symbol is MES1!
  • the timeframe is 5m
  • the date range covers Jan 2019 – Dec 2025
// STEP 10 — COMPARE THE OUTPUT

look at the Metrics tab in the strategy tester. compare your output against the published baseline:

if your output matches all five metrics exactly, you've reproduced the baseline. you're done.

if your output doesn't match, see the troubleshooting section below.

// TROUBLESHOOTING

if your output doesn't match the published baseline, work through these checks in order. one of them is the answer.

  1. SYMBOL — confirm you're on MES1!, not ES1!, MNQ1!, or any other symbol. the front-month continuous micro S&P contract.
  2. TIMEFRAME — confirm 5m. not 1m, not 15m. the baseline is computed on 5-minute bars.
  3. DATE RANGE — confirm January 1, 2019 through December 31, 2025 (inclusive). a one-day shift on either end will change the trade count.
  4. SESSION TIMES — confirm session start 09:00, session end 10:00, ORB cutoff 09:35, exit time 16:00, timezone America/New_York.
  5. RISK PARAMETERS — confirm target risk $250, max risk per trade $350, max contracts 40. mismatched risk values will change position sizes and therefore P/L.
  6. ENTRY MODE — confirm "ORB" only. if your settings dialog shows "Open + Stretch" or any other mode, you're either using a Pro indicator or you've changed the default. reset to ORB.
  7. INDICATOR VERSION — confirm you have the published v2.2 Free, not an older version. the script title in the chart legend should read "ORB v2.2 [ORB Alpha] - Free".
  8. DEFAULT SETTINGS — try clicking "Defaults" in the indicator settings dialog to reset everything, then re-apply the session times, risk parameters, and date range above.

if you've worked through all eight checks and your output still doesn't match, the most likely cause is that TradingView has updated the way it caches historical data and your local cache is stale. try removing the indicator, re-adding it, and re-running the strategy tester.

if it still doesn't match after that, contact ORBAlpha and we'll help you find the difference.

// WHAT THIS BASELINE TELLS YOU

a 50% win rate with a 1.07 profit factor on 1,629 trades over 7 years is a positive-expectancy system with a modest edge. it's not a get-rich strategy — it's a disciplined, mechanical execution of one specific entry pattern (opening range breakouts) with strict risk management and no discretion.

the modesty is the point. ORB v2.2 Free is the open core. it gives you the engine, the discipline, and the audit trail without any of the filters, fades, or recovery logic that the Pro edition adds. it trades more often, takes more setups, and has a lower win rate than the Pro version because it doesn't reject low-quality setups.

ORB v2.2 Pro layers a configurable filter stack, the ORF (Opening Range Fade) reversal engine, redemption logic for missed setups, and late failure detection on top of the same execution core. Pro takes fewer setups, rejects more low-quality entries, and trades a higher win rate at meaningfully better profit factor on the same window.

when Pro launches, this guide will have a sister guide that walks through reproducing the Pro baseline. side-by-side comparison will make the upgrade case visible.
