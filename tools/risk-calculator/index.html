// RISK CALCULATOR

position sizing for traders who actually want to survive.

// WHAT IT DOES

the risk calculator uses the Kelly Criterion to compute your optimal position size given your edge and your capital. for prop trading accounts, it uses a non-standard capital input — your maximum allowed drawdown, not your account balance. this is the difference between a Kelly calculation that helps you and one that blows up your account.

// WHY THIS IS DIFFERENT

every generic Kelly calculator online asks for your account balance and tells you to risk a percentage of it per trade. for a $50,000 prop account with a $2,500 static drawdown, standard Kelly might recommend risking $12,500 per trade. the first losing trade ends the account.

the problem is not Kelly. the problem is the input. Kelly's math assumes the capital you give it is capital you can actually lose without the game ending. for a prop trader, the game ends at the drawdown limit — not at zero. the capital Kelly should size against is the drawdown, not the account balance.

this tool asks for your maximum drawdown as the capital input. the Kelly math runs against that number. the output is position sizing that respects the real constraint: the drawdown limit is the account, everything above it is an accounting fiction.

// HOW TO READ THE OUTPUT

the calculator outputs four numbers. each is a fraction of Kelly's mathematical optimum.

FULL KELLY — the mathematical optimum. do not use this number. full Kelly assumes your edge estimate is exactly right, that you can tolerate 50%+ drawdowns, and that you have infinite time to let the math play out. none of these are true in real trading. full Kelly is shown here as a reference point, not as a recommendation.

HALF KELLY — Ed Thorp's recommendation for traders who can tolerate significant drawdowns. captures roughly 75% of Kelly's growth rate with roughly half the drawdown. aggressive. suitable for traders with large samples of live data confirming their edge and high tolerance for variance.

QUARTER KELLY — the recommended starting point. captures roughly 56% of Kelly's growth rate with about a quarter the drawdown. this is where most disciplined traders land. use this unless you have a specific reason to go higher or lower.

EIGHTH KELLY — conservative. captures roughly 23% of the growth rate with drawdowns roughly 1/8th of full Kelly. use this when your edge estimate is based on a small sample, when market conditions are uncertain, or when you are in the early phase of a prop evaluation and cannot afford any drawdown volatility.

each output also shows the number of consecutive losing trades your capital can absorb at that size. if this number is less than 10, the sizing is likely too aggressive for the amount of variance normal trading produces.

// WHEN NOT TO USE THIS

Kelly only works when your edge estimate is real. this tool will happily compute a position size based on garbage inputs. do not use the output if:

  • your win rate and average win/loss are based on fewer than 100 trades. edge estimates below this sample size have error bars wide enough to make Kelly dangerous.
  • your edge was measured on in-sample backtest data only. Kelly assumes the edge will repeat. over-fit backtests do not repeat.
  • you cannot explain why your strategy has edge. if you cannot articulate the reason, you probably do not have edge — you have a lucky sample.
  • your capital input is your full account balance. this tool assumes you are entering your maximum drawdown, not your balance. if you enter the wrong number, Kelly's output will be wrong by a factor of ten or more.
  • you are in the first 30 days of a prop evaluation and have not yet established a profit cushion. during early evaluation, drawdown tolerance is effectively zero. use Eighth Kelly or smaller.

if you cannot satisfy all five of these conditions, this tool is not the right sizing method for your situation. use a flat 0.5% of drawdown per trade as a safer default and focus on building the sample and edge validation this tool requires.
